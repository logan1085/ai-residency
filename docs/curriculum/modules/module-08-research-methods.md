# Module 8: Research Methods

Module 8 turns curiosity into disciplined research practice: reading, replication, ablation, and communication.

## Learning Objectives

- Read papers for claims, methods, assumptions, and evidence.
- Reproduce a small result from a recent paper.
- Design ablations that isolate causal factors.
- Write research memos with clear uncertainty.

## Subtopics

- Literature search and citation graphs.
- Paper reading templates.
- Reproduction scope and compute constraints.
- Ablation design.
- Statistical caution and negative results.
- Research memos and peer review.

## Assignments

- Build an annotated bibliography of ten papers.
- Select one paper claim to reproduce.
- Define minimal success criteria before running experiments.
- Write a memo comparing expected and observed results.

## Projects

- Reproduce a small benchmark result.
- Extend a paper with one new ablation.
- Present a five-minute research briefing.

## Proof of Mastery

- Annotated bibliography.
- Reproduction plan with constraints.
- Experiment log and result table.
- Research memo with limitations and next steps.
